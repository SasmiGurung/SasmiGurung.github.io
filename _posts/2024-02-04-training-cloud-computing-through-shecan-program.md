According to the Labor Force Survey of Nepal in 2017–2018, the economically active female population over 15 years old was 81%, but only 0.5% were in the ICT sector. With this underrepresentation and under-participation of Nepalese girls and women in the technology landscape, I had always dreamt of empowering and uplifting the female community of Nepal through my contributions.

In 2023, for the first time, AWS User Group Nepal launched a program “She Can” which is especially targeted at female undergraduate, graduate students, or fresh graduates for their skill and career development. The She Can program offers mentorship to seven girls, one from each province. It had a duration of 4 months with 48 plus sessions directly from industry experts and trainers. This program aims to guide the mentee to earn AWS Cloud Academy Foundation Badges and Certifications. Along with this, guidance is provided for the AWS Certifications Exams and opportunities for job placements. Among the mentors, I was one of the mentors providing the mentees with hands-on practice and resolving the technical questions.

![mentor](/images/blog-images/she-can/mentor.jpg)

The She Can program is an excellent opportunity for Nepalese females who are seeking to develop their skills and careers in Cloud Computing. As for me, I was involved as a Cloud Practitioner Trainer, providing guidance and help for the AWS Academy course. I also gave demonstrations of the AWS Services such as EC2, Networking, IAM, and so on. Then they had the opportunity to practice hands-on on their own. With this approach, the mentees were able to understand the theoretical concepts as well as able to execute the hands-on activities as well. As for me, mentoring these girls, was an opportunity to fulfill my urge to contribute back to the community. Along with this, I further enhanced my skills in mentoring and coaching.

From the participation of the girls in this program, I found that the technology community of females in Nepal is very enthusiastic and active. They just require an opportunity and platform like “She Can” to demonstrate their capabilities and learning ability. While the education system, mostly focuses on theoretical knowledge and outdated content, the female community of students and graduates requires more than that to increase their participation. I am hoping that more opportunities and events will follow such as this program to cater to the skill and career development of the amazing, strong, and talented girls and women of Nepal.
