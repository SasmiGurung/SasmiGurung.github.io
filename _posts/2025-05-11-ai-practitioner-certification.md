As AI has been increasingly influencing software development and redefining DevOps role, learning AI has been on my professional roadmap. After building a solid foundation with AWS Cloud, I decided to explore how artificial intelligence fits into the bigger cloud ecosystem. That’s when I came across the AWS Certified AI Practitioner certification which helps to validate foundational AI and ML knowledge in the AWS environment.

In this blog, I’ll share how I prepared, what resources helped the most, and a few tips for anyone planning to take this certification next.

## What the Exam Covers
This is a foundational-level exam (similar to the AWS Cloud Practitioner), with 65 multiple-choice and multiple-response questions. The exam duration is 90 minutes and you need at least 700 score out of 1000 to pass the exam.

The AWS Certified AI Practitioner exam tests your understanding of:

+ Fundamentals of AI and ML (20% of scored content)
+ Fundamentals of Generative AI (24% of scored content)
+ Applications of Foundation Models (28% of scored content)
+ Guidelines for Responsible AI (14% of scored content)
+ Security, Compliance, and Governance for AI Solutions (14% of scored content)

## My Study Plan & Resources
I dedicated around 4 weeks of focused study time for this exam, since it’s beginner-friendly but still concept-heavy. Here’s what I used:

+ AWS Skill Builder Course: “AWS Certified AI Practitioner Official Exam Prep” — free and straight from AWS experts.
+ Hands-On Labs: I explored AI services like Amazon Bedrock, SageMaker JumpStart, and Rekognition using my AWS account.
+ Udemy Course: Ultimate AWS Certified AI Practitioner AIF-C01 by Stephane Maarek
+ Udemy Practice Mock Question: [Practice Exams] AWS Certified AI Practitioner — AIF-C01 by Stephane Maarek

## My Preparation Strategy
As I didn’t have experience in AI, I started with the basics by learning the fundamentals of AI and ML terminology. When I experimented with hands-on AWS AI Services and their respective use cases, the concepts of AI and ML became more clear. In my opinion, understanding Responsible AI was easy but solving questions was really tricky. So, I studied in-depth about fairness, bias, and transparency in AI. Furthermore, a time based mock exam from Udemy helped me to get familiarized with the exam system and question types.

## Exam Day Experience
I took my exam at an authorized test center. The process was smooth with a quick ID verification, a locker for personal items, and then I was guided to my testing booth.

The exam questions were straightforward but conceptual which were meant to test understanding more than memorization. Many questions described real-life scenarios where you have to identify the right AWS service or AI concept.

My advice: read carefully and think about use cases. For example, which AWS service fits text-to-speech, image recognition, or chat interactions.

## The Moment I Passed
When the email results mention “Congratulations!”, I felt genuinely proud. Even though it’s an entry-level certification, it represents my evolving step toward combining cloud knowledge with AI understanding.

This certification gave me confidence to explore deeper into machine learning, generative AI, and data analytics within AWS.

## Final Thoughts
Earning the AWS Certified AI Practitioner credential was a rewarding experience. It opened the door to understanding how AI integrates with AWS cloud solutions and how organizations can use these services responsibly.

If you’re thinking about starting your AI journey, this certification is the perfect first step which is approachable, relevant, and deeply connected to the future of cloud computing.

Keep learning, stay curious, and remember: AI is only as powerful as the creativity and responsibility we bring to it.
